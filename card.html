Wolf

The wolf feeds predominantly on wild herbivorous hoofed mammals that have a body mass similar to that of the combined mass of the pack members.

  • Scientific name: Canis lupus
  • Average Length: 105-160 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 6-8 years (in the wild)
  • Habitat: Wilderness and remote areas

Its populations consist of packs and lone wolves, most lone wolves being temporarily alone while they disperse from packs to form their own or join another one. Cortisol levels in wolves rise significantly when a pack member dies, indicating the presence of stress.
